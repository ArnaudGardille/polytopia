Water, Carry, Stiff, Static

The **Raft** is the most basic naval [unit](</wiki/Unit> "Unit"). Moving a land unit onto a [port](<Port.md> "Port") will turn it into a Raft. The health of a Raft is determined by the unit it carries. 

A Raft can be upgraded into a [Rammer](</wiki/Rammer> "Rammer") for five [stars](</wiki/Stars> "Stars") once [Aquaculture](<Navigation.md> "Navigation") is researched, a [Scout](</wiki/Scout> "Scout") for five [stars](</wiki/Stars> "Stars") once [Sailing](</wiki/Sailing> "Sailing") is researched, or a [Bomber](</wiki/Bomber> "Bomber") for 15 stars once [Navigation](<Navigation.md> "Navigation") is researched. Upgrading a naval unit does not heal it. 

The Raft replaced the [Boat](</wiki/Boat> "Boat") for the [Path of the Ocean](</wiki/Path_of_the_Ocean_Beta> "Path of the Ocean Beta") update. With regards to its stats, it is identical to the boat except without the ability to attack. It is used only as a transport unit.

## Strengths and Weaknesses[[](<https://auth.fandom.com/signin?redirect=https%3A%2F%2Fpolytopia.fandom.com%2Fwiki%2FRaft%3Fveaction%3Dedit%26section%3D1&uselang=en> "Sign in to edit")]

The Raft is good for: 

  * **Starfish harvesting**. Especially in tribes with easy or instant access to ocean tiles, Rafts can be sent out to harvest Starfish in the background of gameplay. However, stronger units should be used if hostile tribes are nearby.
  * **Transporting units.** Placing a unit in a Raft is free, making them useful for transporting units across continents. However, bridges are a more efficient yet expensive way of crossing singular water tiles.

The Raft isn't good for: 

  * **Combat**. With no attack, the Raft is useless in battle. It should always be upgraded in a city before it is sent to enemy lines, since it will be unable to do so outside city borders.

## Trivia[[](<https://auth.fandom.com/signin?redirect=https%3A%2F%2Fpolytopia.fandom.com%2Fwiki%2FRaft%3Fveaction%3Dedit%26section%3D4&uselang=en> "Sign in to edit")]

  * Before the beta that added the Forgotten tribe skin, Rafts did not display shading on their sails. 
    * Before this version, the ₼idŋighţ raft had a simpler appearance.
  * Before the [Patch of the Ocean](</wiki/Patch_of_the_Ocean> "Patch of the Ocean") update, the roof of a Raft was always tan and did not change color based on its tribe. 
    * Before beta update 2.8.0.11434, this roof was a darker tan color.
    * The Aumux tribe skin's raft still used the darker tan color until beta update 2.9.2.12491, and the Ragoo skin continued to do so until the beta that added the Forgotten skin.
