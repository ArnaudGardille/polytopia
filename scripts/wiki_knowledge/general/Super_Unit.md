A **super unit** is a [unit](</wiki/Unit> "Unit") obtained primarily by upgrading a [city](</wiki/Cities> "Cities") to level 5 or above. 

Each [tribe](<Tribes.md> "Tribe") can only spawn one type of super unit. The super unit of all regular tribes is the [Giant](</wiki/Giant> "Giant"); each special tribe has its own unique super unit. 

Although a super unit cannot be trained in a city using [stars](</wiki/Stars> "Stars"), all super units have a theoretical cost of 10 stars. This is reflected in a super unit's information card, [Disband](</wiki/Disband> "Disband") value of 5 stars, and [point value](<../game_mechanics/Score.md> "Score") of 50 points. (A unit's point value is five times its cost in stars.) 

## Strategy[[](<https://auth.fandom.com/signin?redirect=https%3A%2F%2Fpolytopia.fandom.com%2Fwiki%2FSuper_Unit%3Fveaction%3Dedit%26section%3D1&uselang=en> "Sign in to edit")]

A super unit is very strong. A common strategy in [multiplayer](</wiki/Multiplayer> "Multiplayer") games (especially on smaller [map sizes](<../game_mechanics/Map_Generation.md> "Map Generation")) is to "rush" a super unit, trying to obtain it as quickly as possible. 

Spawning a super unit in a besieged city will push out the invading unit. This is known as a [forced unit spawn](<../game_mechanics/Movement.md> "Movement"). Most super units are well-suited for defending a city because most super units have high health; the exception is the [Dragon Egg](</wiki/Dragon_Egg> "Dragon Egg"), which can be destroyed easily.
