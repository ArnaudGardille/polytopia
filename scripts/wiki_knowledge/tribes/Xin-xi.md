> They start their journey in the dense mountains, surrounded by beautiful cherry blossoms.
> 
> Tribe description

**Xin-xi** is the first [tribe](<../general/Tribes.md> "Tribe") in Polytopia. It is a free regular tribe, and it starts with the [Climbing](</wiki/Climbing> "Climbing") [technology](</wiki/Technology> "Technology"). 

Xin-xi has a moderately slow start, being unable to harvest any resources immediately, but their bonus to ore spawn rates combined with increased mountain spawn rates allow them to consistently rely on mines and decent-size forges to grow their cities in the mid-game. These mountains also afford Xin-xi a decent amount of protection, giving defence bonuses to all Xin-xi units on mountains, and forcing enemies to research Climbing to fight Xin-xi on equal terms. Xin-xi also has an easier time exploring their own biome with the increased vision from standing on mountains, allowing them to discover ruins and villages in their biome more easily and removing some need to take an early [explorer](</wiki/Explorer> "Explorer") from their second or third cities to reveal the map. Xin-xi overall is a decent pick in most land maps, where they can benefit fully from their mountain bonuses and exploit them for early protection.

## Strategy[[](<https://auth.fandom.com/signin?redirect=https%3A%2F%2Fpolytopia.fandom.com%2Fwiki%2FXin-xi%3Fveaction%3Dedit%26section%3D5&uselang=en> "Sign in to edit")]

    _Main article:[Xin-xi/Strategy](</wiki/Xin-xi/Strategy> "Xin-xi/Strategy")_

Xin-xi do not start with a population tech so you cannot upgrade on T0, however because of [climbing](</wiki/Climbing> "Climbing"), they can scout out very early to find villages, [ruins](</wiki/Ruins> "Ruins"), water and enemies. If you find ruins, take it if it is in a convenient location. If you have metal in your territory, go for [Mining](</wiki/Mining> "Mining") and [Smithery](</wiki/Smithery> "Smithery"), as this will help you build a decent economy going into the game. If you do not have metal in your territory, go for whatever is best, e.g. [Fishing](</wiki/Fishing> "Fishing") or [Hunting](</wiki/Hunting> "Hunting"). Capturing a city is useful, but remember to get any techs you need beforehand. You will want to use scouting on mountains to your advantage as much as possible. 

The Xin-xi are one of the few tribes for which it is viable to research [Smithery](</wiki/Smithery> "Smithery") and use [Swordsmen](</wiki/Swordsman> "Swordsman"). Starting with the [Climbing](</wiki/Climbing> "Climbing") technology allows Smithery to be researched more quickly, and more mountains lead to more metal resources that can be harvested through [Mining](</wiki/Mining> "Mining"). Swordsmen can be powerful troops, with high attack and defence, while being far less expensive as [Knights](</wiki/Knight> "Knight") (especially since the technology required to unlock Knights, [Free Spirit](</wiki/Free_Spirit> "Free Spirit") and [Chivalry](</wiki/Chivalry> "Chivalry"), typically serve no other use). 

## Inspiration[[](<https://auth.fandom.com/signin?redirect=https%3A%2F%2Fpolytopia.fandom.com%2Fwiki%2FXin-xi%3Fveaction%3Dedit%26section%3D6&uselang=en> "Sign in to edit")]

The Xin-Xi tribe appears to be inspired by Japanese and Chinese cultures ([cherry blossoms](<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cherry_blossom> "wikipedia:Cherry blossom"), mountains resembling [Mount Fuji](<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mount_Fuji> "wikipedia:Mount Fuji")). Their helmet design resembles Japanese [Kabuto helmets](<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kabuto> "wikipedia:Kabuto") used by samurai, but they could also be inspired by Chinese helmets.

> Sha-po skin description 

[![ShapoSplash](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](<https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/supertribes/images/a/aa/ShapoSplash.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20230401202131>) [![ShapoSplash](images/ShapoSplash.webp)](<https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/supertribes/images/a/aa/ShapoSplash.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20230401202131>) [](</wiki/File:ShapoSplash.jpg>)

The splash screen for the Sha-po skin in the Tribe Selection menu.

The Sha-po skin was added in the second skin pack for Polytopia. They wear black hoods covering most of their face and shoulders instead of the Xin-xi helmet, with a black bandana and the two red accents from the Xin-xi helmet still present. 

  * The city buildings are mostly the same in terms of structure, but with the red roofs recolored to black.
  * The monuments' green elements are also recolored to black.
  * The Warriors and Riders use long wooden staffs instead of clubs.
  * The Archers throw _[shuriken](<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shuriken> "wp:Shuriken")_ instead of a bow and arrow (their sprites originally mistakenly included quivers, but this has since been fixed)
  * Their Swordsmen and Giants use longer, _[katana](<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Katana> "wp:Katana")_ -like swords.

## Trivia[[](<https://auth.fandom.com/signin?redirect=https%3A%2F%2Fpolytopia.fandom.com%2Fwiki%2FXin-xi%3Fveaction%3Dedit%26section%3D17&uselang=en> "Sign in to edit")]

  * Although [Imperius](</wiki/Imperius> "Imperius") is the first and original tribe, Xin-xi is the default tribe for the code and is tribe "0" in the code.
  * The Xin-xi are inspired by East Asian cultures, especially Japanese culture. The 2019 Tribe Day blurb is about Mt. Xin-Xi, which may be a reference to Mt. Fuji, and the architecture in the 2020 Tribe Moon poster seems inspired by Japanese architecture. It bears a strong resemblance to the Chureito pagoda, a famous pagoda in front of Mt. Fuji.
  * The Xin-xi theme was originally named "Synthetic Disco."[4]
  * The "leader" of the Xin-xi tribe is named "Shayoszu".
