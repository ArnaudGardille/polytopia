> The Luxidoor love expensive things, jewels, rare spices and exclusive ornaments. That is also why they are the most expensive tribe in Polytopia!  
> The Luxidoor citizens lead very comfortable lives, draped in the finest purple silk. But will they survive outside of their beloved capital?
> 
> Tribe description

**Luxidoor** is the seventh [tribe](<../general/Tribes.md> "Tribe") in Polytopia. Luxidoor is the only regular tribe that does not have any starting [technology](</wiki/Technology> "Technology"); instead, they start with a level 2 capital [city](</wiki/City> "City") (without a [Workshop](</wiki/Workshop> "Workshop") or [Explorer](</wiki/Explorer> "Explorer"), and so giving +3 spt). A [Park](</wiki/Park> "Park") graphic can also be seen, however it is purely cosmetic and does not give any points. 

Luxidoor is a flexible mid-tier tribe which has the advantage of being able to use their starting stars to choose a tier 1 tech to suit their city's resources while also still having a level 2 capital by the end of t0. Additionally, they require less resources in their city to reach level 5 and get a Super Unit from their capital, as they start with two population already "harvested".

## Inspiration[[](<https://auth.fandom.com/signin?redirect=https%3A%2F%2Fpolytopia.fandom.com%2Fwiki%2FLuxidoor%3Fveaction%3Dedit%26section%3D5&uselang=en> "Sign in to edit")]

The Luxidoor are inspired by Indian culture.[5]

The Luxidoor tribe's turbans and elephants are common motifs in various Indian cultures.

[![AumuxSplash](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](<https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/supertribes/images/0/02/AumuxSplash.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20230401203736>) [![AumuxSplash](images/AumuxSplash.webp)](<https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/supertribes/images/0/02/AumuxSplash.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20230401203736>) [](</wiki/File:AumuxSplash.jpg>)

The splash screen for the Aumux skin in the Tribe Selection menu.

The Aumux skin was added in the second skin pack for Polytopia. They wear a modified turban with a golden mask covering their whole face, golden crests on top, and a slightly larger green gem. 

  * The grass is entirely absent in Aumux fields and mountains, revealing only the black soil underneath with gold specks dotted on it.
  * The Warriors, Riders, Archers, Swordsmen, and Giants all have golden versions of their original weapons.
  * The Catapult is also made entirely of gold instead of wood, and uses giant green jewels as projectiles instead of rocks.
  * Naval units all have gold replacing their wooden parts.
  * They use the default city buildings, with only the fifth city building replaced by an all-gold one.

  * In one early iteration of the Aumux skin, they had the name "Gôlyloki" and their description read, "With skills in battle matched only by their exorbitant fees, Gôlyloki mercenaries function as the personal army of the Luxidoorian Emperor."[6]

## Trivia[[](<https://auth.fandom.com/signin?redirect=https%3A%2F%2Fpolytopia.fandom.com%2Fwiki%2FLuxidoor%3Fveaction%3Dedit%26section%3D16&uselang=en> "Sign in to edit")]

  * The Luxidoor [Easter egg](</wiki/Easter_Eggs> "Easter Eggs") gives players that do not own Luxidoor a small chance of meeting the tribe as an opponent in single-player games. This is officially an “apology” for Luxidoor being more expensive than other tribes. Before [Moonrise](<../general/The_Battle_of_Polytopia__Moonrise.md> "Moonrise"), in single-player, disabling all but one tribe would force Luxidoor to be the opponent due to this Easter Egg, even if the player did not own Luxidoor.
  * Luxidoor "inherited" their trees from [Kickoo](<Kickoo.md> "Kickoo"). Before the release of Luxidoor, Kickoo had the trees that Luxidoor now has.
  * In update 1.5 (Dawn), there existed three unused initial designs for Luxidoor's forest.[7]

[![](images/Unused_Luxidoor_forest_3.png_\(26_KB\).webp)![](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D)](</wiki/File:Unused_Luxidoor_forest_3.png> "Unused Luxidoor forest 3.png \(26 KB\)")

  * In [update 2.0.49](<../general/Update_History.md> "Update History"), Luxidoor was nerfed from starting with a level 3 capital with a [City Wall](</wiki/City_Wall> "City Wall") to starting with a level 2 capital. Prior to this update, Luxidoor was widely considered one of the best tribes in the game. The level 3 capital produced 4 stars per turn, allowed Luxidoor to produce more [units](</wiki/Units> "Units") and outnumber nearby enemies in the early-game, and put Luxidoor closer to training an early [Giant](</wiki/Giant> "Giant") than other tribes. However, with this nerf, Luxidoor did gain a cosmetic starting park.[8]
  * The "leader" of the Luxidoor tribe is named "Loniiss".
  * In line with the tribe lore's theme of wealth, Luxidoor is the most expensive tribe to purchase, costing $3.99 USD.
